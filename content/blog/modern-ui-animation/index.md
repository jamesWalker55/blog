## Unfinished

Before I start ranting, let's get some agreement on some key points:

**Key 1: Motion directs attention.**

{{< video src="B4oCN" >}}

Our eyes are attuned to motion. This is a well known basic fact, and UI designers take advantage of this by making things move when they need your attention.

It's not necessarily bad, and is a good way to direct attention when necessary. But usually it's used in dark UX patterns like a throbbing notification icon to annoy the user into a subscription, but I digress.

**Key 2: You don't read text in motion.**

{{< video src="firefox_75VNO7AaAO_COMP" loop="true" >}}

Have a look at this video above. Most likely, you waited until the text slowed down or stopped moving before you even started reading the text.

Now, I'm not saying it's IMPOSSIBLE to read text when it's moving quickly. However, since you only need to wait a few moments for the text to slow down, most people just wait until then instead of focusing hard and trying to parse the text within the first few moments of the animation.

In other words, you don't read the text until the animation is over.

With both key points combined, we get the conclusion: **Moving things are both attention-seeking and unreadable**.

It's heartbreaking when you've spent weeks and months on a cover song, only for customer support to reject your song for "being a remix".

## "Covers" vs "Remixes"

(This is not legal advice)

Covers are arrangements of the original song, but without using any audio samples from the song. Cover songs are allowed on streaming platforms like Spotify and more, though download stores like Apple Music are more complicated. There are also some country restrictions like the US, but under certain circumstances, you are allowed to distribute cover songs **without a licence**.

Remixes on the other hand do use audio samples from the song. No license will allow this, you need explicit permission from the original song author.

## Shitty customer support

You never realise how important customer support is until you need it. I've needed customer support several times, which is why I can say Amuse has absolutely dog shit customer service.

The main thing is the response times. You email them and they'll respond a several days later with the most useless copy-and-pasted response. I spent blood and sweat making my music, having to email some dickhead on a 2-day ping just makes me want to tear my hair out. You'd be lucky to even hear back from them.

Releasing cover songs is a gamble every time. If you're unlucky and get a braindead reviewer that doesn't know the difference between a cover and a remix, your release gets rejected and you get a strike on your account. Get enough strikes and I assume my account will be closed.

## Research your distributor for drama on forums

Lately I've found a good way to check for drama for potential distributors I'd like to switch to:

Search `[distributor name] scam site:reddit.com` on Google and `[distributor name] scam` on YouTube.

Distributors that have poor customer service and shady practices tend to have more complaints from users online. I did a quick search for DistroKid and now I've decided to avoid them like the plague too.
